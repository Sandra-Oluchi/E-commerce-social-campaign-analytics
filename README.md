# E-commerce-social-campaign-analytics
This is a meta-ad used to drive more sales and traffic to a small to medium-sized e-commerce business.
Business Overview

Plug and Play by MGSN is a Lagos-based online gadget store delivering verified new and pre-owned devices directly to customers. The business differentiates itself through transparency — offering pre-sale device walkthroughs, real-time feature demonstrations, and verified product conditions before delivery.

The core market challenge is trust. Nigerian e-commerce for electronics operates in a low-trust environment where a significant portion of buyers default to in-person shopping at markets like Computer Village in Ikeja, preferring physical inspection over online purchase. Plug and Play's entire value proposition is built around dismantling that hesitation.


Campaign Objective

Objective: Messaging Conversations (WhatsApp/Instagram DMs)

The campaign was set up to drive direct messaging conversations, not website clicks or form submissions. This is deliberate and strategically appropriate for the Nigerian e-commerce context, where most sales are closed through direct conversation rather than a checkout page. Buyers want to ask questions, verify product condition, and confirm delivery before committing. Driving DM conversations is therefore the correct top-of-funnel action for this business model.


Strategic Decisions

1. Creative Format — Video Testimonial

Static image ads were ruled out. In a trust-skeptical market, video is the only format that can:


Show actual device condition in real time
Demonstrate product features and performance visibly
Replicate the in-person inspection experience buyers expect at physical markets


A real customer video testimonial was selected as the creative. This leverages authentic social proof rather than polished brand advertising — a deliberate choice for an audience conditioned to distrust online sellers.

2. Ad Copy — Objection-Led Hook

The opening line directly named the buyer's core hesitation:


"Still walking around Computer Village because you don't trust buying gadgets online?"



Rather than leading with product features or price, the copy acknowledged the objection first. This psychological approach lowers resistance before the body copy builds the trust case. The CTA was kept simple and action-oriented: "Click the link to place your order now."

3. Audience Targeting — Lookalike Audiences

The campaign was seeded from:


Existing one-time and repeat buyers
Active page engagers (comments, reviews, recommendations)


Lookalike targeting matches new audiences to people who already trust and buy from the brand — maximizing conversion probability and reducing wasted spend on an unproven audience with a small test budget.

What This Tells Us

The 100% male response rate is a significant finding. This was not a gender-targeted campaign — it was a broad lookalike audience. The fact that all 6 conversations were initiated by men reveals that:


The current customer base (used to seed the lookalike audience) skews heavily male
The video testimonial creative resonated specifically with male buyers
Either women are not in the lookalike pool in meaningful numbers, or the creative and hook did not speak to female buyers


The multi-generational spread across 18–24, 25–34, and 45–54 suggests the trust barrier around online gadget shopping is not age-specific — it affects buyers across demographics, which validates the objection-led creative strategy.

The 45–54 age group responding is particularly notable. Older buyers are typically harder to convert on social media, which suggests the video testimonial format was especially effective at building credibility with a more trust-cautious audience segment.


Performance Analysis

What Is Working

CTR of 2.91% confirms creative-audience fit. A click-through rate more than 3× the Meta average — achieved on a messaging objective, which typically produces lower CTRs than traffic objectives — is a strong signal. The video testimonial and objection-led hook are resonating with the audience.

CPC of ~$0.09 is exceptionally cost-efficient. Users are engaging with the ad at a very low cost per interaction, meaning the algorithm is successfully matching the creative to an interested audience.

CPM of ₦3,915.83 (~$2.54) is reasonable for a conversion-adjacent objective (messaging) in the Nigerian market. The ad is not fighting expensive auction competition.

Cost per messaging conversation of ₦873.23 (~$0.57) is sustainable relative to the price point of the gadgets being sold. Each qualified lead conversation is being generated at a cost well below typical product margins.

The Key Diagnostic: Clicks vs Conversations Gap

With a 2.91% CTR and 1,338 impressions, the campaign should have generated approximately 39 clicks. Only 6 of those clicks resulted in a messaging conversation being started.

This gap between clicks and conversations is the most important finding in the data. It tells us the ad is not the problem — the creative is working, people are clicking. The friction is happening between the click and the decision to open a conversation. Likely causes:


Landing destination friction: If users are clicking to a Facebook page, website, or product link before being directed to message, each extra step loses buyers
Response time expectations: Nigerian buyers using DM-based purchasing expect near-instant responses; if the page appears inactive or slow to reply, intent drops
Post-click trust gap: The testimonial in the ad builds trust, but if the destination page (profile, website, or DM preview) does not immediately reinforce that same trust signal, hesitant buyers bounce
CTA clarity: "Click the link to place your order" may be creating a mismatch — buyers expect to place an order but land in a conversation flow instead



Decision: Kill, Hold, or Scale?

Verdict: HOLD, Do Not Kill, Do Not Scale Yet

Rationale:

At 1,338 impressions over 5 days with a small budget, this campaign has not exited Meta's learning phase. Meta requires approximately 50 optimisation events (in this case, 50 messaging conversations) before the algorithm stabilises delivery. The campaign generated 6. Scaling budget now would amplify current inefficiencies before the algorithm has learned who to target.

Killing the campaign ignores a 2.91% CTR that clearly signals the creative and audience are aligned. That signal is valuable and should not be discarded.


Recommended Next Steps

Priority Action Rationale 
1. Audit the post-click journey, identify exactly where users are dropping between click and message — this is the bottleneck
2. Simplify the CTA to "Send us a message. "Reduce friction; align expectations with the actual campaign objective

Key Takeaways for Stakeholders


The creative is working — a 2.91% CTR on a messaging objective is strong by any benchmark
The audience targeting logic is sound — lookalike seeding from existing buyers produced relevant, engaged traffic
The conversion gap is a post-click UX problem, not an ad problem — fixing the journey from click to conversation will unlock the campaign's true potential
The demographic insight is actionable — 100% male response on a broad campaign suggests a clear audience skew that future targeting should deliberately address or consciously expand
This campaign should not be judged by 5 days and ₦5,239 — it has produced a valid proof of concept that warrants continued investment with optimisation
